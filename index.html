Helping kids with 3D‑printed hero hands

e‑NABLE is a global network of volunteers who design and 3D‑print prosthetic hands and arms for children in need, at little or no cost to families.

What is e‑NABLE?

e‑NABLE is a volunteer community that shares open‑source designs for 3D‑printed prosthetic hands and arms and connects makers with children and adults who can benefit from these devices.

Thousands of volunteers around the world donate their time, creativity and access to 3D printers to make functional, low‑cost assistive devices for people who might not otherwise be able to afford them.

How e‑NABLE helps families

Step 1

Share your story

Families reach out to an e‑NABLE chapter, explain their needs and send simple measurements and photos, so volunteers can choose the right design.

Step 2

Print and assemble

Makers 3D‑print the parts on desktop printers, add screws, cords and padding, and build a custom device in bright colors the child chooses.

Step 3

Fit and play

The child tries the new hand, learns how to open and close the fingers, and uses it for everyday activities like holding toys, riding a bike or playing games.

Join the e‑NABLE movement

Whether you are a student, teacher, engineer or hobbyist, you can help by printing parts, assembling devices, organizing build events or supporting a local e‑NABLE chapter.

Ways to support e‑NABLE:

  • Use your 3D printer to build hands and arms for recipients.
  • Host workshops at schools or makerspaces to spread the word.
  • Donate materials or funds to help cover printing costs.
